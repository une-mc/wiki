# 3rd Supreme Soviet (July – August 2024)

The **3rd Supreme Soviet** was elected on **11 July 2024** and served until its dissolution on **4 August 2024**, marking one of the **shortest and most unstable legislatures** in post-Rodina Russia.

The chamber consisted of **100 seats**, with a **coalition government** between the **liberal Yabloko party (40)** and the **national-populist LDPR (20)**, together holding a majority of 60 seats. The coalition’s central compromise paired Yabloko’s pro-democratic reform agenda with LDPR’s emphasis on national sovereignty and executive strength.  

The **RSDRP (35)** and **KPRF (5)** formed the **opposition**, criticising the coalition as incoherent and ideologically opportunistic.  

This legislature was defined by **intense factional conflict** and **rapid breakdown of cooperation**. Prime Minister **Xee** (LDPR), appointed as part of the coalition agreement, attempted to centralise power and suppress parliamentary dissent — a move opposed by Yabloko, who viewed it as unconstitutional. The rift led to a **vote of no confidence** on **4 August 2024**, ending both the coalition and Xee’s premiership.  

The collapse of the 3rd Supreme Soviet triggered new elections and ushered in the **4th Legislature**, dominated by the RSDRP–KPRF left bloc.
